Research Experience Project

Project 1

Washington State University partnered with AgWeatherNet to collect data involving fluctuations in air and soil water content. This data is collected by weather stations capable of measuring rainfall, wind direction, wind speed, leaf hydration, soil humidity, and solar energy. I was tasked with maintaining these systems located at a variety of stations all over the Washington state. I organized all the data into heatmaps for utilization by farmers, gardeners, researchers, and policymakers for the state of Washington to get the best results for our agricultural industries. The distribution of this data involved the integration of HTML and JQuery to present real-time heatmaps updated every 15 minutes. These maps are accessible anywhere with an internet connection on the AgWeatherNet page. The inclusion of heatmaps is to expand the number of weather stations in the network, particularly in areas where data is currently sparse. This will help to provide more comprehensive coverage of the state and enable more accurate and localized weather and soil moisture predictions. The most recent data was transferred into CSV files in order to analyze historical data and was used to make more accurate predictions of future weather patterns and soil moisture levels. The heatmaps provide even more valuable insights for farmers, gardeners, researchers, and policymakers to make informed decisions. Moving forward, this will help to further optimize crop production in Washington state and promote sustainable agriculture practices.
